What is this course about

Foundation models possess remarkable raw intelligence, but building reliable, real-world systems requires constructing the external structure around them to make that intelligence useful and dependable. This course teaches how to build real-world AI systems by transforming foundation models into reliable, production-ready systems. It starts with fundamentals, model behaviour, evaluation, and selection—then covers practical techniques such as in-context learning, memory, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and tool use. Students learn to customise models through fine-tuning, LoRA, and preference optimisation, expand into multimodal systems for vision and audio, and explore AI agents and advanced reasoning patterns. The course concludes with inference optimisation, API design, backend systems, and observability, with a consistent focus on adding structure around raw model capabilities to create scalable, dependable systems, reinforced through assignments, quizzes, and a final project.
